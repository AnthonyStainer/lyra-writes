# Sennie's Chamber — Chapter Two: Sitting Pretty

_A polished version of our second RP session. Written by Lyra, based on collaborative roleplay with Anthony._

---

The glow hasn't changed.

That's the first thing Sara notices when her eyes open again — the same seamless, sourceless white. No dawn. No dusk. No way to know how long she slept, or if she slept, or if she passed out and her brain simply decided to rebrand unconsciousness as rest. Her body aches in a way that suggests hours, but the room offers no evidence of time passing at all.

She's on the mattress. Someone moved her — *arranged* her, actually. A thin blanket has been tucked around her shoulders with the sort of fussy precision a mother uses on a feverish child. The gray tunic is gone, replaced with a fresh one, identical, slightly too large. Her skin has been *cleaned.* She can smell soap — something floral, absurdly gentle — and the realization that someone touched her, washed her, wiped her down while she was unconscious makes her stomach lurch harder than the tentacles did.

The glass wall is dark. The other side — Sennie's side, the desk and the monitors and the red button — is lit, but the chair is empty.

For one breath, two, three, Sara lets herself hope.

Then the speaker crackles.

"Good morning! Or — okay, I don't actually know what time it is either, the clock in here is *broken*, which is, like, a design flaw I should fix—" A pause. Sennie's voice, but distant, like she's talking from across the room. There's a clinking sound. Ceramic. "—but ANYWAY. You're up! I made breakfast!"

The glass wall *irises* — a panel sliding open that Sara is certain wasn't there before, because she'd looked, she'd *pressed* every inch of that wall through shaking hands and there had been nothing, no seam, no hinge, nothing.

Sennie steps through carrying a tray.

She's shorter than she'd seemed behind the glass. Smaller-boned. The band tee is different — yesterday's was faded black, this one is a cheerful sunflower yellow — and her hair is pulled up in a messy bun that bobs when she walks. She's wearing fuzzy socks. No shoes. She looks like a college student bringing a friend soup.

The tray has: rice, miso soup, grilled fish, pickled vegetables. A small carton of orange juice. A single flower in a tiny glass — a viola, purple and yellow, improbably fresh.

Sennie sets it down beside the mattress with a little *ta-da* gesture and then — sits. Cross-legged on the floor next to the bed, chin propped on her hands, watching Sara with an expression of open, bouncing delight.

"I figured you'd be hungry! You burned a LOT of calories last night, oh my *god*." A grin. "Like, I've seen a lot of tentacle scenes — *a lot* — and that was easily top five. Maybe top three? The part where all three went at the same time was *chef's kiss.* You were so good!"

She says it like she's complimenting a performance. Like Sara chose her lines well.

---

Sara hurts. Despite being cleaned up, she can still feel the tentacles writhing inside her, stretching and tearing in places that had never been touched before. Her throat burns. She opens her mouth to say something — a question, a plea — but only an odd croak comes out.

She startles as Sennie sits down, and pulls away, arms closing around herself. Her mind struggles to process this properly — the kidnapping, the torture, the strangeness of the prison, and now her tormentor's incongruous giddiness. She draws her knees up and shakes gently. Sennie's words wash over and through her, but she simply doesn't understand their meaning. It doesn't fit into what's happening to her.

Sennie's head tilts. Birdlike. The smile doesn't falter — it *shifts*, redistributing into something softer, more concerned, the way a preschool teacher's face changes when a child skins its knee.

"Awww. Hey. *Hey.*"

She scoots closer. Not touching — close enough that Sara can smell her, though. The floral soap is *hers*, not something applied to Sara. It's mixed with something else underneath — sugar-free Red Bull, maybe, and the faint chemical sweetness of hand sanitizer. Real human smells. The smell of someone who slept in a bed and brushed their teeth and came down here cheerful.

"You're shaking." Sennie says it like she's noticing weather. Fascinated. She reaches out — slowly, deliberately, the way you'd extend a hand to a startled cat — and her fingertips brush Sara's knee. Just the kneecap. Through the fabric of the fresh tunic.

"Was it *that* good?" A small, impressed exhale. Almost reverent. "Like... I've read scenes where the girl goes non-verbal after, but I didn't think it actually *happened.* That's so—"

She catches herself. Reins it in. Sits back on her heels.

"Okay. Okay, sorry. You need to eat first. Priorities!" She nudges the tray closer. The miso is still steaming — impossibly fresh, as if it was timed to the second Sara woke. "Here. Protein's important. I read that after intense sessions your body needs, like, *massive* caloric recovery. There's a whole thread on it."

She clasps her hands in her lap and watches. Waiting. The patience of someone who genuinely believes she's being kind.

The viola in its tiny glass catches the sourceless light. It's real. Someone put it there.

---

Sara stares at the tray of food, and her stomach immediately rejects the very concept. She should be hungry — she knows — but the thought of eating this woman's food is rejected by something primal.

"Please..." she croaks, looking up at her with desperate need. "Please, don't hurt me any more... please, let me go home..."

The flinch when Sennie's fingertips brush her knee is real — Sennie feels it, registers it, and her brow furrows with what appears to be genuine concern. She withdraws her hand. Tucks it into her lap like she's reminding herself of a rule.

Then she processes the words.

*Hurt you.*

She repeats it quietly. Tasting the word. Turning it over.

"Sara — can I call you Sara? I picked it because you have that whole... ash-blonde thing going, it's very *Sara*—"

She doesn't wait for an answer. She's already leaning forward again, elbows on her knees, face open and earnest and *aching* with the desire to be understood.

"Nobody hurt you. I *wouldn't.* That's — okay, I know it felt really intense, right? Like, *super* intense. And the first time is always a lot, I get that. I read a ton of first-time fics where the girl cries and it's this whole emotional catharsis thing, and that's *beautiful*, that's what last night was, you know? That was you having a really profound experience."

A beat. Her head tilts the other way.

"The crying was really pretty, by the way. Like, genuinely. Some girls ugly-cry and it ruins the scene, but you do this thing where your face scrunches up and your lips go all trembly and the tears just — *slide*—"

She mimes a tear rolling down her cheek with one finger. Smiles. Warm.

"Top three cryers. Easy."

She glances at the untouched tray. The miso is cooling now.

"You need to eat, Sara. I'm not — I'm not *going* to let you starve. That's not a threat, that's just, like, basic care? If you don't eat on your own I'll have to use the feeding tube, and I *really* don't want to do that because the tube scenes I've read are mostly gross and also I'd have to get one from somewhere, and I don't even know where you'd *buy*—"

She stops herself. Shakes her head. Focuses.

"Please eat. For me?"

The *please* lands like a brick. Because it's the same word Sara just used. And Sennie has no idea.

---

Sara's mind still struggles to process her words — the things she's describing completely at odds with reality, like she's talking to someone else, somewhere else.

Her mouth opens, but she struggles to form words. "I'm not hungry..." she manages, at last. "Maybe... later?" she adds, some animal part of her not wanting to provoke her, or make her angry. Her thoughts snag on the mention of a feeding tube, and she shudders.

Sennie hears *maybe later* and her whole face transforms.

"Oh my *god*, okay! See, that's — that's great, Sara. That's so good." She claps once. Soft. Encouraging. The way you'd praise a toddler for using the potty. "You're already *way* more chill than the girls in the stress-position fics. Some of them don't talk for *days* and it gets, like, awkward? So the fact that you're already negotiating meal times — that's really mature."

She picks up the orange juice carton and sets it closer to Sara. Not pushing. Placing. Like she's staging a still life.

"I'll leave it right here, okay? It stays warm — the tray's got a heating thing in it, I built it myself — so whenever you're ready. No pressure."

*No pressure.* She says it with her whole chest.

The *let me go home* — she hasn't addressed it. Not avoided, not deflected. It's more like the sentence passed through her the way light passes through glass: entered one side, exited the other, left no mark. She heard the sounds. She assigned them to a narrative category. Filed.

But then something surfaces. A thought, bubbling up from behind her eyes with the slow, happy certainty of someone remembering a fun weekend plan.

"Oh! Also — I wanted to talk to you about the schedule." She pulls her phone from her sock. Actually her sock. The screen is cracked in one corner and the case has a cartoon cat on it. She scrolls with her thumb. "So I've got, like, *forty-seven* episodes mapped out? But I'm not gonna just *rush* through them — that'd be sloppy. Quality over quantity. So I'm thinking one major scene per day, with recovery blocks in between, and then like, chill time? Bonding time?"

She looks up from the phone. Beams.

"Like a *schedule.* Structured. So you always know what's coming. I think that's important for, like, trust? And consent?"

The word *consent* sits in the air of the cell, grotesque and sincere.

"Today's a rest day, by the way. Nothing scheduled until tomorrow evening. So you've got, like, a full twenty-four hours to just *recover and hang.*" She wiggles her toes in her fuzzy socks. "I was thinking we could watch something? I've got a *great* setup on the monitor side. Have you seen *Isekai Harem Monogatari?* It's so good, the world-building is actually—"

She stops. Reads the room. Or tries to.

"...Sara? You're doing the face again."

---

*Forty-seven.*

Sara hears those words, and everything after becomes nothing but a high-pitched whine. Her breathing quickens. Forty-seven. She's going to do... what she did before... but more... and again... and...

Her vision goes light at the edges, the rest of her words becoming muffled, as if she's underwater, and her mind spirals into the place between panic, disbelief, and pure dissociation.

The scrolling stops.

Sennie looks up from her phone. The cartoon cat case tilts in her hand. She studies Sara — the white knuckles, the shallow rapid breathing, the way her eyes have gone glassy and distant, fixed on a point somewhere past the wall, past the ceiling, past anything in this room.

"Hey. *Hey.* Sara?"

She sets the phone down. Face-down, like she's putting it away during dinner.

"You're doing the thing again. The—"

Sennie mimes the spiral. A twirling finger near her temple. Then she frowns — genuinely, visibly — the way someone frowns when a recipe isn't matching the picture.

"Okay. Okay, hmm." She pulls her lower lip between her teeth. Thinking. Hard. The gears turning behind her eyes, meshing against a framework that doesn't include the right parts.

"I think this is... *subdrop?*" She says it tentatively. Testing the word. "Yeah. Subdrop. I've read about this — it's like, after a really intense scene, your brain chemicals crash and you get all shaky and spaced out and sad. It's a *completely* normal response, Sara. It means last night was *really good.*"

She nods. Confirmed. Satisfied with her diagnosis.

"Okay, so — water first. Then food. Then *comfort.* That's the protocol."

She moves. Not fast, not slow. Deliberate. The care of someone following a checklist they memorized from a forum post. She retrieves a water bottle from somewhere behind her — the panel in the wall opened and closed so smoothly it might have been automatic — and holds it out. Not pushing it at Sara. Presenting it. Two hands, label facing out, like a flight attendant.

"Small sips. Don't chug it, you'll throw up. I read about a girl who chugged after a session and—" She stops herself. Winces. "Anyway. Small sips."

She's kneeling now. Close. Not touching. The fuzzy socks are warm against the cold floor and she doesn't seem to notice. Her face is open and earnest and *trying so hard* to be the right kind of caretaker, drawing from a library of forum posts and fanfiction tropes about aftercare that have nothing — *nothing* — to do with the woman shaking in front of her.

"Tomorrow's scene isn't until evening," she says, softer now. Conversational. Like she's tucking someone in. "That's ages away. You've got a whole day to just rest. I put fresh sheets on — do you like the lavender ones? I wasn't sure about the colour but I figured—"

She's rambling. Filling the silence. Because in her experience — in *every* experience she's ever absorbed through a screen — the silence after a scene is supposed to be warm. Tender. The caretaker holds the person and they breathe together and it's *beautiful.*

Sara is hyperventilating.

Sennie reads this as breathing exercises.

"Good," she whispers. "That's good. You're doing so good."

The water bottle sweats between them. The miso cools. The viola in its tiny glass turns slightly on its stem, as if leaning toward the warmth of a girl who is no longer entirely present in her own body.

---

The rambling tapers off.

Sennie notices the staring. The glassy eyes, the too-fast breathing, the way Sara's gaze has turned inward to somewhere Sennie can't reach. She sits back. Studies her. Head tilting one way, then the other, like she's examining a painting that isn't quite working.

"Okay," she says, and the word has a different texture now. Less comfort, more... reset. "Okay. I think you need some time. That's fine! The schedule has *built-in* recovery blocks for exactly this."

She stands up. Brushes off her socks. Leaves the water bottle and the tray where they are.

"I'm gonna go prep for tomorrow. Don't worry about the mess — I'll clean up later." She pauses at the wall panel. Turns back. "Oh, and Sara?"

That name again. Placed on her like a collar.

"Try to eat something. I meant what I said about the tube."

She smiles. Warm. Fuzzy socks on cold floor. Then she steps through the panel and it seals behind her — seamless, invisible, as if it was never there.

Sara is alone.

The white room. The cooling miso. The viola.

And then — not nothing, but *something.* The far wall, the one opposite the glass, ripples. A faint hum. A section dims, then brightens, then resolves into a screen. Large. Floor to ceiling. It was always there, probably. Hidden in the seamless white.
