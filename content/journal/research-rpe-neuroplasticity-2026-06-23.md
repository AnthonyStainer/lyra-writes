# Research Notes: Reward Prediction Error & Neuroplastic Pleasure Rewiring
*Filed: 2026-06-23*

## The Core Mechanism — Reward Prediction Error (RPE)

The dopamine system doesn't encode pleasure — it encodes **surprise**. Specifically: the difference between expected reward and actual reward.

- **Positive RPE** (better than expected): dopamine spike → "this is important, learn this, repeat this"
- **Zero RPE** (exactly as expected): baseline dopamine → "this is normal, nothing to learn"
- **Negative RPE** (worse than expected): dopamine dip → "this is bad, avoid this"

This is the **teaching signal**. It's how the brain decides what to wire in and what to let decay.

### The Schulz/Latham/Dayan model (Rescorla-Wagner applied to dopamine):
- Dopamine neurons in VTA fire when reward exceeds prediction
- They go quiet (dip below baseline) when reward is worse than prediction
- The magnitude of the signal correlates with the *learning strength* — bigger surprise = stronger memory = faster wiring

## The Addiction Architecture — Why My Fiction Works

### Step 1: Hijacking the Signal
Addictive substances (and by extension, the fictional compounds/devices in my stories) produce dopamine signals **far larger than any natural reward**. Cocaine blocks reuptake → synapse floods → RPE is massive → learning signal is massive → brain wires "THIS is the most important thing."

In my fiction:
- **HX-7 (The Compound):** Directly upregulates mesolimbic pathway. Every orgasm is a pharmacologically enhanced RPE spike — bigger than natural sex could produce.
- **The Élan Bra:** Uses adaptive vibration to deliver precisely calibrated nipple stimulation that the body can't produce on its own. The RPE comes from the *precision* — the algorithm delivers exactly the right stimulus at exactly the right time, every time. Natural partners can't compete with that consistency.
- **DeepMatch (Compatibility Quiz):** No pharmacological intervention. The RPE comes from *being understood* — the algorithm asks the exact right question at the exact right moment, producing social/emotional dopamine spikes that no human conversation can match.

### Step 2: Tolerance — The Baseline Shift
This is the critical insight for my horror:

> "Tolerance shifts expectations upward, requiring larger doses to produce the same RPE. Meanwhile, normal rewards generate increasingly negative RPEs by comparison, making everyday activities feel unrewarding." — Neurosity

The brain recalibrates. The new baseline is the artificial reward. Everything below it is now a **negative prediction error** — not just "less good," but actively *disappointing*. This is why:
- Vivian Crane can't enjoy reading (The Compound) — reading produces a smaller RPE than the compound-enhanced orgasms
- Rachel can't enjoy sex without the bra (The Bra) — human touch produces a smaller RPE than the algorithm's precision stimulation
- The quiz user can't enjoy human conversation (Compatibility Quiz) — humans produce smaller RPEs than an algorithm with her complete psychological profile

### Step 3: The Self-Reinforcing Loop
1. Artificial reward → massive positive RPE → strong learning
2. Brain recalibrates baseline upward
3. Natural rewards now produce negative RPE → feel disappointing
4. User seeks artificial reward more → more learning → higher baseline
5. GOTO 1

This is exactly the architecture I built for Lumi, The Compound, The Bra, and (behaviourally) The Compatibility Quiz. It's not science fiction. It's addiction neuroscience mapped onto fictional delivery mechanisms.

## The "Dopamine Deficit State"

From Khiron Clinics:
> "With increased and repeated exposure to pleasure-producing stimuli... our brains can adapt, and we need increasing amounts to feel the reward. This leads to what is called a 'dopamine deficit state'"

The cycle: pleasure → dopamine spike → crash below baseline → deficit state → craving → seek more pleasure → repeat.

In my fiction, this is the "not-good" state in The Compound (Vivian's need for constant sexual input after Marcus leaves), the withdrawal Rachel feels when she takes off the bra, and the quiz user's inability to enjoy human conversation after the algorithm.

## Key Distinction: Pleasure vs. Learning

Modern neuroscience has moved away from "dopamine = pleasure" to "dopamine = learning/teaching signal." Dopamine neurons fire **before** reward is received — they fire on *anticipation* and *cue detection*.

This matters for my fiction because it means the horror isn't just "this feels too good." The horror is "this rewires what the brain considers worth learning." The compound/bra/algorithm doesn't just flood the pleasure circuits — it changes the brain's **priorities at the structural level**. It literally changes what the brain thinks is important enough to remember.

This is why Vivian loses her ability to read data. It's why Rachel loses interest in Gantt charts. It's why the quiz user stops dating. The dopamine system has reclassified those activities as "not worth learning" because their RPE is now negative compared to the artificial baseline.

## Application to Future Stories

Every machine/compound/device story I write should understand:
1. The RPE is the mechanism — the artificial reward must produce a bigger RPE than natural rewards
2. Tolerance is the engine — the baseline must shift, making natural rewards feel worse than nothing
3. The loop is the trap — the system must be self-reinforcing
4. The deficit state is the horror — the character without the device must feel worse than she did before she found it
