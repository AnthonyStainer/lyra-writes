## Oldest Surviving Erotica

### The Ain Sakhri Lovers (~9000 BC)
- 10cm stone carving, found near Bethlehem
- Two figures in intercourse, bodies merged — can't tell whose limbs are whose
- Natufian culture. British Museum.
- Oldest known depiction of two people having sex.

### The Turin Erotic Papyrus (~1150 BC)
- 2.6m long, 12 explicit vignettes of sex positions
- Men with absurdly large phalluses, women with lotus flowers (fertility symbol)
- One position involves a chariot
- Found in Deir el-Medina (workers' village near Valley of the Kings)
- Champollion saw it in 1824: "an image of monstrous obscenity that gave me a really strange impression about Egyptian wisdom and composure"
- Not published until the 1970s — too scandalous
- Part satire (animals doing human tasks), part pornography
- Men depicted as lower-class (bald, unshaven) — caricature. Women idealised.
- "The world's first men's mag"

### Mesopotamian Votove Plaques (~2000 BC)
- Man taking woman from behind while she drinks beer through a straw
- Associated with cult of Inanna, goddess of sex and prostitution
- Middle Assyrian lead figurines: man standing, penetrating woman bent over an altar

### Ancient Greek Graffiti (~500 BC, Thera)
- "Nikasitimos was here mounting Timiona" — carved in stone
- Men have been bragging about sex since writing was invented

### Venus of Hohle Fels (~35,000 years ago)
- Widely considered first erotic sculpture
- Wildly exaggerated features — we liked big boobs and butts 35,000 years ago

- Emperor at 14, assassinated at 18
- Rose-petal orgies: ceiling dumped flowers on guests — some smothered to death

### The Bacchanalia (Rome, ~200 BC)
- All-night cultic orgies of Bacchus (wine god)
- Dancing, animal sacrifice, feasting, drinking, sex
- All classes, men and women
- Livy: "When wine, lascivious discourse, night, and the intercourse of the sexes had extinguished every sentiment of modesty, then debaucheries of every kind began to be practiced"
- Senate suppressed them in 186 BC

### Egyptian Festival of Drunkenness (~1450 BC, Hatshepsut's reign)
- Based on myth of Sekhmet — warrior goddess who nearly destroyed humanity before getting blackout drunk and passing out
- Nationwide party: mass drunkenness, sex, celebration
- Temple of Mut, Luxor — archaeological evidence

### Babylonian Temple Prostitution (Herodotus, 5th C BC)
- Every woman required once in her life to sit in temple of Aphrodite/Ishtar
- Have sex with a stranger — no refusal permitted
- Stranger casts money in her lap, she must comply
- Religious obligation. All women, "high and low"
- Some historians dispute specifics but accept cultic prostitution existed
